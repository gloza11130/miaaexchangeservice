Beginner's Guide to Setting Up a Bitcoin Wallet

A quick guide to get you started:

Choose a Wallet

Select a reputable wallet. Popular options include mobile apps (Trust Wallet, Exodus), exchanges (Coinbase, Binance), or ultra-secure hardware devices (Ledger, Trezor).

Create & Secure It

Download the app or sign up, then write down your 12-24 word recovery seed phrase. Store this offline in a safe place—it is the key to your funds. Enable 2-Factor Authentication (2FA).

Get Your Address

Inside your wallet, find the "Receive" function to generate your unique public address (it will look like a long string of letters and numbers). This is what you share to receive Bitcoin.

Buy Bitcoin

You can purchase Bitcoin using a credit/debit card or bank transfer on exchanges like Coinbase, Binance, or Kraken. You can also use Bitcoin ATMs or peer-to-peer platforms.

Store Safely

Never share your seed phrase. For large amounts, transfer funds from an exchange to your personal wallet for added security.

For funding your account, you can search for "Bitcoin vendors" or "exchangers" in your local region to find a trusted provider.
